The Restaurant

An Oasis of
Refined Calm

Situated at 155 West 51st Street in Midtown Manhattan, Le Bernardin offers an atmosphere of serene elegance. The room — warm, unhurried, and precise — mirrors the philosophy of its kitchen: nothing superfluous, everything essential.

Designed to make every guest feel the quiet privilege of exceptional hospitality, Le Bernardin's dining room has been a sanctuary for those who understand that true luxury lies in restraint.

Our Commitment

Sustainably
Sourced, Always

We are committed to purchasing and serving only responsibly sourced seafood — without compromising the future of our oceans. Every ingredient on our menu is chosen with the same reverence we bring to its preparation.

Our partnerships with sustainable fisheries span continents. The result is a menu that changes with the seasons, guided always by what the ocean can generously and responsibly provide.

The Menu

A Language of
Preparation

At Le Bernardin, every dish belongs to one of three philosophies — a language that describes our relationship with the ingredient, and our respect for its natural form.

I
Almost Raw

The fish barely touched by heat. Pure expression of the ocean's finest — refined by curing, marinating, or the gentlest of preparations. The ingredient speaks for itself.

Bluefin Tuna · Caviar · Nori Crisp
Yellowfin Tuna · Foie Gras · Toasted Baguette
Kampachi · Kumquat · Jalapeño
II
Barely Touched

Delicate heat applied with absolute precision. The flesh yields only at the moment of perfection — translucent at the center, gently yielding to the fork.

Poached Halibut · Truffled Morels
Dover Sole · Romanesco · Cauliflower
Wild Striped Bass · Mushroom Broth
III
Lightly Cooked

The fullest expression of technique — where classical French mastery meets the elemental simplicity of the sea. Cooked through, yet never surrendering its essential character.

Pan-Roasted Grouper · Wild Mushrooms
Grilled Salmon · Mushroom Vinaigrette
Seared Skate · Capers · Brown Butter

The Chef

Eric
Ripert

Born in Antibes, France, Eric Ripert has led Le Bernardin's kitchen since 1994. His approach is deceptively simple: honor the fish, master the technique, remove everything superfluous. The result has earned three Michelin stars — every year since the guide began awarding them in New York.

A recipient of the James Beard Award for Outstanding Chef, Ripert has defined an entire school of cooking — one that holds the ocean in reverence and places the ingredient, not the chef, at the center of every plate.

"When you walk into my kitchen, you feel the respect we have for the ocean. That respect is the foundation of everything we do."
The Kitchen

Mastery Through
Discipline

Twelve years of continuous three-star recognition do not happen by accident. They happen through a kitchen culture that demands and delivers absolute consistency — every service, every plate, every day.

Our brigade is led by some of the most accomplished professionals in American fine dining. Their collective discipline, curiosity, and dedication to craft form the backbone of every meal served here.
